Army Time

The Time I spent in the Army

I did my basic training in Ft Jackson, and my AIT at Ft Sill. After all the training I was sent to Ft Bragg. I was Bragg for about 3 years, with a year of that being delpoyed. Living on a mulitary base came with some challenges, such as hearing gunfire and artillary fire randomly throughout the day or at night, and sometimes living in the field for up to a month if not longer. One of my funniest experiences while in the Army was driving back from New Mexico to North Carolina after a 2 week exercise. it was a 3 day trip on a bus. The bus I was on got pulled over because the charter company we used was known for smuggling in drugs on their buses. It was hilarious when the officer got into the bus and asked if we were carrying firearms, and the person in charge told him we were carrying over 30 M4 rifles, a couple of grenade launchers and a handful of M9s.

During my time at Ft Bragg I was deployed to Kuwait and then Bahrain for about a year. During that time I was transfered from 1-7 HHB to 1-7 Alpha. However before i transfered I did a wall with HHB. At the end of my deployment with Alpha I did another wall so I can happily say I left my name in two places in Bahrain. During my time in Bahrain I worked 24 hour shifts every other day, and met the local wildlife. Unfortunately I lost the picture of the second wall I did with Alpha, but I have a picture of my wall that I did with HHB.

Some Skills I picked up in the Army

  • Ability to remain calm and perform in a stressfull situation
  • Lead and work with a team of 3-4 people
  • Train and evaluate different tasks and drills
  • Strategic and organizational Planning
  • Ability to communicate, especially under duress
  • Ability to be responsible for, and maintain equipment worth hundreds of millions of dollars
  • Operate across multiple continental boarders
  • Recieve and analyze critical information while under time and other constraints
